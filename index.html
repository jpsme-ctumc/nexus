Welcome to Project Nexus

As the second semester approaches, Third-Year BSME students face the critical transition to their On-the-Job Training (OJT). Historically, our mechanical engineering students encounter significant challenges during this phase.

These challenges include the logistical burden of the search, the "hassle" of blind hunting for companies, and the disadvantage of lacking a "backer" or professional network. Furthermore, there is often uncertainty about documentary requirements and limited knowledge of stipends or allowances.

Our Initiative

To address this, the Internal and External Affairs (IEA) Committee proposes Project OJT NEXUS. This centralized directory serves as a channel for gathering intelligence from 4th Year students and Alumni to guide incoming trainees. By sharing verified company details, application modes, and firsthand experiences, we aim to ensure equal access and a smoother OJT journey for every aspiring mechanical engineer.
